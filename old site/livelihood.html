PROVIDING SOLAR-POWERED WATER, FARMING INPUTS AND EDUCATION TO UNDERPRIVILEGED WOMEN IN ZAMBIA


Many small scale farmers in the rural parts of Zambia depend on season farming to sustain their families. Over the last few years, climate change has disrupted the way of life, unpredictable rain patterns make it hard for people to engage in farming thereby making it hard for them to provide for their families. Covid-19 has also contributed to the already worse situation.

Working together to protect one another is our way of life and with this, we aim to set up communal Solar Powered Water Systems for Irrigation and safe drinking water, to provide drip irrigation kit to cover 50*50m area for Individual family farming plots, Provide vegetable production inputs for one successful cycle and to Conduct trainings in Agribusiness, financial literacy, water management and conservation, climate-smart agricultural practices.

With a donation from as low as $10, you can help improve the livelihoods of the many struggling rural families particularly women and children especially in the face of COVID 19. An estimated 7.6 million people in Zambia are socio-economically affected directly and indirectly by the pandemic. For rural communities, this challenge adds to pre-existing Nutrition and Food security challenges which have been caused by poor harvests due to unpredictable rainfall (climate change). Your donation will enable Africa Access Water through the ‘Women Scaling up Nutrition through Solar-powered Water Project’, to empower and equip the families with necessary skills and resources to setup for all year vegetable farming that is linked to viable markets, subsequently increasing income and ensuring food security.
